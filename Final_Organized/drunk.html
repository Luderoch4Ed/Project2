Total Accident vs Drunk Driving Over Month

This Line chart shows the y axis as the total number of accidents reported during the year in multiples of 1000 (2,400 = 2.4M accidents for example). The blue line represents drunk drivers while the the green line represents the total count of accidents in any given month. We see the months displayed on the bottom of this chart. Drunk driving occurs most often in the Summer months between May-August, with the highest peak in June and July. This is another example of how weather conditions actually appear to have less to do with the rate of accidents than we might assume.

Total Accident vs Drunk Driving By State

This Bar chart further emphasizes the data from the Heatmap which we shared earlier. We can see that several outlier states including: Florida, Texas, California and Georgia have the highest rates of accidents overall. Warm weather, dense population centers and lots of vehicles on the road are all factors which can be observed.
