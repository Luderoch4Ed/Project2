US Accidents Project Overview


US-Accidents can be used for numerous applications such as real-time accident prediction, studying accident hotspot locations, casualty analysis and extracting cause and effect rules to predict accidents, or studying the impact of precipitation or other environmental stimuli on accident occurrence.
The dataset covers 49 states of the US. Our group examined data from the NHTSA which was extracted from Kaggle.com, cleaned and uploaded to MongoDB. We then created a RESTful API using Flask and PyMongo to call the MongoDB database and perform queries on the data. Our interactive visualizations can be used to determine which factors are most important to consider when examing the root causes of traffic accidents within this study.

    • Continental US Map Overview

    The Heatmap reveals that larger cities with higher population densities tend to be trouble spots for getting into accidents. You can see several areas such as Los Angeles, Dallas/Houston, Atlanta, New Jersey and the D.C. Metro area are clear examples of this. However, population is not the only factor to examine because there are large cities such as New York and Chicago which don't seem to have as great a number of accidents. One possible explanation could be that the people in those cities rely on public transportation vs driving themselves to a greater degree.
