Countrywide Accident Summary


Executive Summary

Our accident analysis to date has included a partial picture of behaviorial and conditional factors. We have been able to generally rule out inclement weather as a significant factor and that holds true as we look into the data more deeply year over year. We have observed that larger city centers where warm weather is more prevalent, typically result in a higher rate of incidents or 'hot spots'. However, there are many more underlying factors to explore further and interestingly enough, even the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration does not seem to have all of the answers. For example, when it comes to reporting on accidents where fatalities were involved, approximately 30% of all reported incidents (2018) were cited as 'unknown'.

Administrative Summary

The National Highway Traffic Safety Administration has developed a list of driver behaviors that are factors in crashes. Speeding is at the top of the list of factors for drivers involved in crashes overall. As an example, in 2018, 8,596 drivers who were involved in fatal crashes (or almost 17 percent) were speeding. Ranking second was the influence of alcohol, drugs or medication. Coming in third and fourth were failure to stay in the proper lane, and failure to yield the right of way.
