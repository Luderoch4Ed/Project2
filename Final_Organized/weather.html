Weather Factors-- Temperature VS Visibility

The Scatter chart shows the y axis as visibility(miles) compared to the x axis as the temperature(F). Anywhere between 9 miles to 11 miles of visibility, we notice that accidents greatly increase. The same is true when temperatures are higher during the year, anywhere from around 65-95 degrees. We can see that despite ideal weather conditions, people get into a greater number of accidents. This data also reveals that the opposite is true when driving conditions are not the most ideal. A couple of possible explanations is that people are just more careful when operating their vehicles in colder months with less visibility or perhaps there might just be far less people on the road during those times of the year.
